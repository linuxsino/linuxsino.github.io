Research

My research focuses on the deep integration of artificial intelligence and psychological behavioral science, centered around the core theme of "Trustworthy Affective Computing." I have established a comprehensive research framework spanning computational infrastructure, multimodal trustworthy understanding, and implicit emotion modeling.

1. Computational Infrastructure for Non-verbal Psychological Intelligence

Pioneering identity-free micro-gesture and micro-movement video data systems based on psychological theories of non-verbal subconscious micro-behaviors, establishing a new paradigm of "identity-unrecognizable yet emotion-sensitive" psychological decoding.

A15iMiGUE: The First Micro-gesture Emotion Understanding Benchmark

Systematically collected over 18,000 natural interaction segments from post-match interviews with nearly 100 athletes across 28 countries, focusing on emotion cues from subconscious muscle movements with strong anti-spoofing characteristics.

A14SMG Dataset: Psychological Stress State Analysis

First synchronized collection of individual psychological stress states and micro-gesture change signals, advancing fine-grained modeling for non-contact mental health monitoring.

A6FSBench: Artistic Performance Psychology Dataset

First emotional expression video dataset for figure skating, systematically analyzing emotional expression in sports performance art and audience emotional resonance.

Impact: These datasets constitute two-thirds of the core foundational resources in international subconscious micro-behavior research and have become the de facto standard testing benchmarks. Supported by multiple key patents (PT3, PT5, PT8) covering identity removal, micro-action modeling, and cross-modal emotion consistency verification.
"Highly evaluated by international scholars including Prof. Cynthia Breazeal (AAAI Fellow), Prof. James Z. Wang (ACM Fellow), Prof. Michelle G. Newman (APA Fellow), Prof. Tsuhan Chen (Singapore Academy of Engineering), and Prof. Björn W. Schuller (Editor-in-Chief of IEEE Trans. Affective Computing) for its potential in understanding genuine emotions, assisting psychological assessment, and improving AI emotional sensitivity."

2. Trustworthy Multimodal Emotion Understanding in Large Models

Addressing three critical challenges in AI emotion understanding: input authenticity, identity-independent reasoning, and emotional hallucination assessment.

A11DiffFAS: Generative Diffusion for Face Anti-spoofing

First introduction of generative diffusion models to face anti-spoofing tasks through spatio-temporal progressive denoising mechanisms, significantly enhancing emotion analysis system security in cross-domain testing.

A2DEEMO: De-identity Multimodal Emotion Reasoning

Breakthrough framework integrating identity information stripping with multimodal emotion reasoning, achieving input person identity desensitization while preserving emotional expression context consistency.

A5CAT+: Emotion Synergy in Multimodal LLMs

Introducing emotion synergy mechanisms to model emotional resonance processes across facial, vocal, and textual modalities, enhancing psychological consistency and cross-modal robustness.

A1EmotionHallucer: Emotional Hallucination Assessment

World's first evaluation system focused on emotional hallucination phenomena in large models, systematically assessing 38 mainstream models and establishing quantifiable hallucination degree indicators.

Technical Defense Systems: Reconstructed Vision Transformer and Masked Autoencoder architectures (IJCV 2024), consistency regularization methods (TIFS 2023), and domain-modality dual alignment frameworks (ICCV 2025) to address cross-domain challenges.

3. Implicit Emotion Modeling: From Micro-visual Signals to Autonomic Decoding

Establishing a complete pathway from micro-visual signal capture to physiological rhythm modeling and implicit psychological state recognition, based on psychophysiological mechanisms of autonomic nervous responses.

A7Period-LLM: Multimodal LLM for rPPG Modeling

First introduction of AI large models into rPPG modeling pipeline, significantly improving extraction accuracy and cross-scene adaptation of non-contact physiological signals including heart rate variability, respiratory patterns, and blood flow dynamics.

A13rPPG-MAE: Self-supervised Micro-visual Modeling

Utilizing self-supervised micro-visual modeling strategies to enhance rPPG signal stability and individual generalization capability.

A9Vision Transformer for Subtle Target Detection

Pioneering simultaneous detection theoretical framework for salient and implicit targets, advancing subtle emotion signal recovery.

A20LSD: Low-rank and Structured Sparse Decomposition

ESI highly cited paper providing fundamental modeling framework for implicit signal analysis, widely cited by international leading scholars including Academy Fellows Dacheng Tao, Wenwu Zhu, Xuelong Li, and IEEE Life Fellows Mubarak Shah, Matti Pietikäinen.

Patent Portfolio: Multiple core patents (PT2, PT6, PT9, PT12) covering rPPG signal extraction, micro-visual behavior perception, subtle target enhancement, and temporal rhythm reconstruction, enabling end-to-end modeling from perceptual signals to psychological mechanisms.
