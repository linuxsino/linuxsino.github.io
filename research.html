Research

My research focuses on the deep integration of artificial intelligence and psychological behavioral science, centered around the core theme of "Trustworthy Affective Computing." I have proposed and implemented a comprehensive psychological understanding system that spans from data collection to model construction, and from input security to output trustworthiness. This work advances emotion recognition from laboratory paradigms reliant on identity and explicit facial/speech signals toward de-identified, ethically-aware, psychologically-consistent, and practically-oriented general recognition frameworks.
Research Vision: To develop AI systems that understand human emotions in a trustworthy, ethical, and psychologically-grounded manner, enabling applications in mental health, human-computer interaction, and public safety.

Key Research Directions

1. De-identified Affective Computing

Developing emotion recognition systems that operate without relying on identity-revealing facial or vocal features. This approach preserves privacy while maintaining accurate emotional assessment through micro-behavioral analysis and physiological signals.

Key Innovation: First comprehensive framework for identity-free emotional state analysis using micro-gestures and micro-movements, addressing privacy concerns in real-world applications.

2. Input Authenticity and Security

Implementing robust anti-spoofing mechanisms through liveness detection, micro-expression dynamic consistency analysis, and temporal physiological signal verification. This ensures systems can distinguish genuine human emotional expressions from synthetic or manipulated inputs.

Technical Focus: Multi-modal authenticity verification combining facial action units, micro-expressions, and physiological signals for reliable input validation.

3. Psychology-Guided AI Reasoning

Integrating established psychological theories—such as subconscious micro-behavior analysis and facial action unit-emotion mapping—into large AI model architectures. This transforms emotion recognition from black-box classification to psychologically-constrained causal understanding.

Theoretical Foundation: Systematic embedding of psychological constraints in AI reasoning processes, enhancing model interpretability, controllability, and psychological consistency.

4. Trustworthy Output and Ethical Control

Addressing emotional "hallucinations" in large models through causal chain-constrained correction mechanisms and ethical intervention modules. This ensures AI emotional feedback maintains ethical consistency and psychological credibility.

Ethical Framework: Novel approaches for identifying and correcting unreasonable emotional judgments or potential manipulation risks in model outputs.

Research Impact

The integrated research framework establishes a complete trustworthy loop—from input source control through reasoning process modeling to output result correction and ethical control. This three-in-one approach provides theoretical support and technical pathways for sustainable development of AI emotion recognition in critical domains including:

  • Mental Health: Objective assessment tools for psychological well-being and emotional disorders
  • Human-Computer Interaction: Ethical and psychologically-aware interactive systems
  • Public Safety: Reliable emotional state monitoring in security-critical environments
  • Education: Adaptive learning systems responsive to student emotional states

Future Directions

Current research efforts are expanding toward:

  • Cross-cultural emotional intelligence in AI systems
  • Real-time trustworthy emotion recognition for dynamic environments
  • Integration of neurophysiological signals with behavioral analysis
  • Development of standardized benchmarks for trustworthy affective computing
  • Ethical frameworks for emotion AI deployment in sensitive applications
